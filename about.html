Our Story

NayePankh Foundation started during the most difficult times of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. Seeing the devastating impact of lockdowns on daily-wage earners and marginalized communities in Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh, a small group of high school students decided they couldn't stand by.

Pooling their own pocket money and gathering essential items, they began distributing food packets and sanitization materials to families living in local slums. What began as a tiny, direct-response initiative soon grew into a massive volunteer effort.

To scale this impact and establish a legal infrastructure of trust, the NGO was officially registered under the UP Government in March 2021. Today, NayePankh has grown into a highly active, student-led NGO mobilizing thousands of young citizens to uplift India’s underprivileged communities.

The Youth Movement

At NayePankh, we believe that India's youth possess the maximum energy and potential to solve grassroots problems.

By maintaining a 100% student-run coordination committee, we ensure that administrative overheads are kept under 5%, deploying nearly every single rupee of support directly to food drives, textbooks, and hygiene campaigns.

Mission, Vision & Values

The principles that direct our student operations, campaign launches, and donor relations.

Our Mission

To uplift underprivileged communities through quality education, healthcare awareness, nutritional food support, clothing drives, and social service.

Our Vision

A future where every child and family has access to opportunities, self-reliance, and basic human dignity, regardless of their social origins.

Our Values

  • Compassion: Serving with empathy.
  • Service: Commitment to action.
  • Integrity: 100% financial transparency.
  • Youth Leadership: Harnessing student energy.
  • Community Impact: Sustained local changes.

Our Growth Timeline

Discover how a localized student campaign expanded into a sustainable multi-city foundation.

2021

Foundation Started

NayePankh Foundation is officially registered under the UP Government. Initial teams establish food distribution networks in local Kanpur slum blocks.

2022

Expansion

We launch structured women’s menstrual hygiene camps, distribute over 15,000 sanitary napkins, and receive Section 12A/80G tax-exempt certifications.

2023

More Community Programs

Launch of Child Education sponsorship kits and street library programs. Daily meal drives are scaled to feed hundreds of homeless families and stray animals.

2024

Large Scale Volunteer Network

Expansion of college chapters across Uttar Pradesh and nearby states. Our active student volunteer network crosses 10,000 members, driving massive local collection drives.

2025+

Continued Growth

Scaling digital computer literacy training, establishing permanent stray rescue routes, and setting up vocational training programs for rural women.
